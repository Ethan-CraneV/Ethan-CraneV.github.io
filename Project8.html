This "hand prosthetic" as it was called was an assignment given as part of the McMaster Engineering 1 Cornerstone Design Project. The project gave a set of design specifications which included the following:

1. A specific gear ratio of 12:1 from the input gear to the output gears.
2. The output (finger and thumb) axles were constrained to a specific spatial distance away from the input axle, as defined by the prefabricated prosthetic frame.
3. The tips of the finger and thumb had to meet when closed.
4. The assembly had to fit within a prefabricated prosthetic frame which was provided.

The gear ratio is 2:1 * 2:1 * 3:1 * 1:1 * 1:1 The axles and hex nuts were provided. The frame was assembled using hot glue and super glue, and post-print modifications were made by melting the material with a soldering iron to get the gears to mesh correctly. These modifications were needed due to the precision errors that arose during the 3D printing process.
